My Story

Wandering miles

Inspired by Roald Dahl’s notable novel George’s Marvelous Medicine, this webpage and its name reflects a blend of my love for research and reading. In 8th standard I received an assignment to present over Microbes, I chose Fungi. Powered by curiosity and fueled by my computer with internet access I dived quite deep and turned in enough information to publish a book - okay maybe that’s an exaggeration! But indeed, I explored high school biology on this quest, laying the foundation of my academic journey.

My nerd self found solace in books during school, so much so that I am sure my Amortentia would smell of an Old Library (Potterheads assemble ⚡). Fiction, classics, encyclopedias, atlases, and biographies, I had them all covered. That is when I must have stumbled first upon Wings of Fire, Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam’s autobiography, positioning him as my idol for life.

As a single child I knew how to keep myself busy without being the MMA moves test subject for a sibling. This meant rolling on my skates, learning, dancing, or thinking in my spare time. I always had the wildest ideas to turn science into something productive. When I was introduced to the concept of energy I found it silly not to use all that walking on roads to produce power. My joy knew no bounds when I later found out about the piezoelectric tiles in Japan that function on this concept.

Eventually my passion started to take form as a BSc Honors in Microbiology from University of Delhi. For about 3 semesters, COVID-19 played its part and my classes remained confined on a screen. Yet when I qualified for two summer internships simultaneously, I felt relieved for the opportunity of performing laboratory work. At Dr. B. R. Ambedkar Centre for Biomedical Research I first entered a professional scientific setting, and absolutely loved it. From brushing up on my molecular biology techniques to understanding the intricacies of mammalian cell culture, the Oncology lab proved extremely kind to me. The fellow interns, lecture seminars from esteemed scientists, mentorship from Prof. Daman Saluja and insightful discussions with PhD seniors validated my inquisitiveness and cemented my zeal to pursue research.

Just as I concluded my internship here, I moved to Bangalore for two months of adventure. As a JNCASR Summer Research Fellow I was provided accommodation in India’s best research institute, a legacy of Mr. Jamsetji Tata, the Indian Institute of Science. My first opinion of the grand campus was astonishment. Sunlight filtering through the tall majestic trees spread across 400 acres was intimidating. Travelling to and fro NIMHANS and my guest house in IISc was the least challenging part of my tenure. As a 19 year old who had never treaded farther from home, I faced hurdles beyond what I fathomed. But like a phoenix I emerged as a different person, with more wisdom, clarity, knowledge and friends.

As destiny would have it, I was called by the divine city of Kashi to continue my MSc in Applied Microbiology from Banaras Hindu University. A hostel as a home and dreams in my eyes, I partook the course in the spirit of science. And as I eventually left for Jawaharlal Nehru University to work on computational drug discovery as a part of my Masters Thesis, I carried with me bittersweet memories and lasting bonds. My dissertation was beyond my expertise in the wet lab because I hold faith in Dr. Kalam’s words - “If you want to leave your footprints on the sands of time, do not drag your feet.” Initially I struggled with my usual micropipettes and culture plates being swapped with computer keys and codes. But it gradually grew on me and I conquered the digital realm of biology in a tiny sense.

As I await and venture in the next steps of my voyage I have come up with this webpage to project my learnings to a wider world. Maybe someday some 11 year old girl would believe she can do science because Saumya could!

With dreams in eyes and dares in heart,
Microbially yours,
Saumya
